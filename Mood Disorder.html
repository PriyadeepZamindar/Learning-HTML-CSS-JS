Lithium

Salts: Carbonate/Citrate. 1597mg Li2co3 has 300mg elemental Li. Levels: 0.6-1.2 mEq/L. Dosing - 600-900 in divided doses. Not metabolised. Only renal excretion. T1/2 about 24 hours. T/eq-5-7 days

Excreted in Breast milk. A/w Ebstein's anomaly. (First trimester mostly)

S/E: Renal- Polyurea with secondary polydipsia(25-35% - use K sparing diuretics like spironolactone, amiloride). Non Specific Interstitial Fibrosis- decreased GFR. Have to do RFT -6 months.

S/E: Thyroid:Goitre(in 5%), Hypothyroidism(7-10%). TFT - 6monthly.

S/E: Neurological: Postural tremors(8-12 Hz). Toxicity: Coarse tremors, dysarthria, ataxia, impaired consciousness, fasciculations, myoclonus, Sz, coma. Sometimes Cognitive dulling.

S/E: Skin - maculopapular rash, worsening psoriasis, alopecia. Cardiac - Mostly benign - hypokalemia like. Some wt gain.

Lithosun, Licab. About Rs 20 / 10 tablets.



Valproate

Formulations: Valproic acid, Divalproex Na: 1:1 of valproic acid and sodium valproate, Na Valproate Injection. Antimanic conc: 50-150 microg/ml. Dosage:oral- 20-30mg/kg/day. 1200-1500mg/day

t1/2: 10-16 hours. Favourable thyroid and renal profile. Metab by hepatic. Starts acting within 1-4 days. Better than Li for irritable mania, children, elderly, rapid cycling. Better for agitation than dysphoria.

S/E: HATS-P: Hepatotoxicity, Hyperammonimea, A/w Urea Cycle, Teratogenicity- Neural Tube Defect, Thrombocytopenia, Somnolence in Elderly, Pancreatitis.

S/E: Wt gain, Alopecia, PCOS(atleast 33%), moderate hyponatremia.

Torvate(300) about INR 30/10tabs. Dicorate, Divaa about INR 150-200/10 tabs of 500mg each



Lamotrigine

Stabilising BPAD from below. Not very effective in mania.T1/2-25hours. Excreted in urine. Dose: 100-200 mg/day. Relatively safe in pregnancy/lactation. MOA: voltage sensitive Na -

monothrapy: 25mg/2 weeks increase to 100-200. With CBZ 50mg/2weeks. With Valproate- 25mg/4weeks.

Weak 5HT3-, serotonin reuptake inhibitor, modulates release of glutamate and aspertate. Lengthening span b/w depressive episode.

S/E: Relatively well tolerated(lacks sedation/wt gain/metabolic). Benign maculopapular rash(8%). SJS/TEN in 0.1%

Drug interactions: Valproate doubles plasma lvl. Lamotrigine decreases Valproic acid. Decreased conc with CBZ,phenytoin, phenobarbital.



CBZ and Oxcarbazepine

Second line. OxC is analogue of CBZ. T1/2 CBZ- 18-54hrs. Chronic-autoinduction.T1/2-12hrs. Metabolized in liver. MOA- VDSC,NMDA receptors, Alpha1-, VDCC. Mostly used in acute mania.

S/E: Well tolerated(no wt gain). Serious s/e- blood dyscrasias(aplastic anaemia, agranulocytosis) - routene hemat monitoring 3,6,9,12 months.

S/E: Maculopapular rashes(rare SJS/TEN). Neural Tube defect in pregnancy. Secreted in breast milk. Drug interactions- many CYP3A4 induction.

Antimanic dose: 1200mg/day. anticonvulsant conc: 4-12microg/ml. Oxcarb has increased risk of hyponatremis(3-5%). Less risk of blood dyscrasias and rashes.

T1/2 OxC - 9-12 hrs. Dose range - 150-2400 mg/day. B/D dosing.
